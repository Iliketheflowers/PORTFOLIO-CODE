View the world through my perspectives

I travel a lot, and photography is usually how I slow myself down while doing it. Wherever I am, I'm drawn to the people who live and work there, the ones shaping a place through small, everyday actions. I like observing how people move, interact, rest, and exist within their surroundings.

Alongside that, I photograph nature, animals, and landscapes, not in a dramatic, postcard way, but in moments that feel honest and unpolished. Sometimes it's about light, sometimes about texture, sometimes just about something being visually satisfying for reasons I can't fully explain.

My images come from curiosity more than intention. I don't arrive with a shot list. I respond to what's around me. If something feels visually strong, calm, strange, or quietly beautiful, that's usually enough.

Below, you'll find a selection of my favorite photographs from Bangkok, Bayombong, and Boracay.

Small Project about Movement

This small photography project explores how movement and narrative can exist within still images. By placing images next to each other, I tried to create a sense of flow — allowing the viewer to move through the story rather than presenting a single, fixed moment.

The photographs don't aim to explain everything, but to invite interpretation, imagination, and that tiny “wait… what's happening here?” feeling.
